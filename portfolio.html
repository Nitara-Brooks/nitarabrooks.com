2025 Graphic Design Portfolio



In Bloom

2024

Branding for the spring 2024 Graphic Design BFA Senior Exhibition at Appalachian State University. With this collaborative brand identity, we aimed to create a sense of childlike joy and growth as these designers moved from the comfort of college to the “real world.” Assigning a system of flower icons, each artist and project was easily identifiable.

I worked mainly with production, printing the vinyl, stickers, and supplementary material.




Music as a Means of Living

2025

Inspired by a sudden realization at a concert, Music as a Means of Living is an exploration of experiences and goals I look forward to in life. Music is a powerful tool and has helped me work through feeling/ expressing emotions. It is not the theme of this project but the catalyst for an extensive line of thinking. I’ve created five posters to help embody my findings based on the themes of Music, Love, Hate, Discovery, and Independence.




Hullabaloo

2025

An imagined branding for a new in-print company under Huzzah Books, located in Boone, North Carolina. In collaboration with Megan Adylette, we created a playful framework, composed of monochrome patterns, allowing for a flexible and versatile brand that grows and evolves with the conversations Hullabaloo inspires.

Hullabaloo is an in-print company that focuses on unconventional lost media. We create a space for play and meaningful connections through reviving old literature. Our publications inspire conversations on identity, language and communication.




Black American Gothic

2025

Inspired by a fascination for the Southern Gothic, this poetry book, “Black American Gothic: The Critique of a Country Built on Hate,” came to life. It embodies the struggle black Americans have faced for centuries. When we think of Gothic literature, we picture monsters, mutilation, and horrors beyond belief. It was never a metaphor for the black populous but a reality. The books leads readers to understand what goes on, why, and what they can do to help.




Bivvy

2025

Imagined branding and call to action for a website, envisioned by Amalie McCracken, that helps people find activities to do around their local areas. I specifically worked on the branch for Boone, North Carolina.

Bivvy believes in the excitement and spontaneity of life! New experiences are always just around the corner, especially in your local community! With our amazingly curated quiz, you can find a random adventure to spice up your daily routine!

Tailey Bone

2024

Through limited Illustrations, I depicted the Appalachian folk tale Tailey Bone. I developed a blocky shape-driven illustration style that implements overlaid fabric texture, giving the images more depth. I paired the drawings with a typeset retelling of the story, formatting it as an accordion-style book.
